    I learned the general scope of the Gestalt theory which is that the human brain will always find a way to simplify complex designs by seeing structures and patters within them. This can be divided into 6 principles: Proxomity, common fate, continuity, similarity, closure, common region, and symmetry. Proximity refers to how close two elements are, common fate are for elements that get grouped together because they point to or are moving in the same direction. Continuity refers to how our eyes will always follow the smoothest lines, and similarity is used to group elements into the same catagory becaues of their color, shape, size, etc.. Closure refers to how out minds will fill in gaps of a design that aren't explicitly there (for examle the WWF panda logo <- even though it's not a full panda, the shapes are so similar that our brain will fill in the gaps). And much more...
